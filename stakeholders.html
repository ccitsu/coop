Field Training Employer's Role

The employer plays a crucial role in the success of the program.

Pre-Training Guidelines

  • 1. Advise the CCIT Training Unit about available coop training positions.
  • 2. Assign a Field Supervisor (mentor) to each student. The mentor should be a professional in the student's subject area.
  • 3. Provide the student with a work plan for the training period that is related to their academic field. The Faculty Advisor must approve this plan.

Guidelines during Training

  • 1. Treat the student trainee like any other employee.
  • 2. Report student absences in evaluation reports.
  • 3. Encourage students to prepare technical reports and presentations.
  • 4. Allow and encourage site visits by the Faculty Advisor.
  • 5. Submit a sealed and stamped copy of the company's final student evaluation report at the end of the program.

Post-Training Guidelines

  • 1. Provide a training certificate to students who successfully complete the program, specifying the trainee's position and tasks.
  • 2. Fill out the "Employer Satisfaction Online Survey."

University's Role

The university, through its different stakeholders, collaborates with the employer to ensure the program's success.

CCIT Training Unit

Pre-Training: Leads in organizing training, collects a list of departmental coordinators, contacts companies to solicit positions, and assigns qualified students to positions based on qualifications and preferences.

During Training: Maintains regular liaison with employers and mentors, and coordinates with department coordinators to resolve any issues.

Post-Training: Collects annual field experience reports and ensures satisfaction surveys are administered.

Field Training Coordinator

Pre-Training: Organizes a meeting with students and advisors to explain the program, allocates training locations, assigns faculty advisors, and prepares the calendar for presentations.

During Training: Resolves issues between students, advisors, or supervisors, and reports final grades after collecting all evaluation forms.

Post-Training: Administers satisfaction surveys, evaluates the achievement of learning outcomes, and is responsible for the quality report.

Faculty Advisor

Pre-Training: Reviews and approves the student's work plan and submits a copy to the coordinator.

During Training: Conducts at least one mandatory site visit to the training organization, resolves issues, and reviews and provides feedback on student progress reports.

Post-Training: Guides students in writing the final report and preparing their presentations, and submits their final evaluation to the coordinator.

Field Training Examining Committee

The committee, consisting of the Faculty Advisor and two other faculty members, evaluates the student's final report and presentation. The committee reports the grade to the Field Training Coordinator.

Key Success Factors

The field training program's success depends on active collaboration between:


Employers
Providing meaningful work experience

University
Coordinating and supporting the process

Students
Engaging actively and professionally
