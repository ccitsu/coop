Glossary

Field Training

A structured educational experience that integrates classroom theory with practical, on-the-job training. This is a mandatory component for all academic programs in the CCIT.

CCIT Training Unit

The university body responsible for coordinating and managing the field training program, including liaising with external companies.

Field Training Coordinator

A faculty member appointed by the department to oversee the program, assign advisors, and manage student applications and evaluations.

Faculty Advisor

A faculty member assigned to a student to provide guidance, approve work plans, and evaluate progress throughout the training period.

Field Supervisor

An employee at the host organization designated to supervise the student trainee, provide a work plan, and conduct performance evaluations.

Field Training Examining Committee

A committee composed of the Faculty Advisor as chair and two other faculty members who evaluate the student's final report and presentation.

Introduction

Three Major Stakeholders:

  • 1. Coop students
  • 2. Coop employers
  • 3. The university (CCIT)

The College of Computing and Information Technology (CCIT) has established the field training program as a capstone activity of its curriculum. This program aims to create a strong link between these major stakeholders.

By participating, students gain a pre-professional learning experience, applying theoretical knowledge in a real-world setting. This handbook serves as a guide for all stakeholders involved in the program, setting forth the standards, guidelines, and procedures to ensure a successful and meaningful experience for students.

CCIT Programs:

The College of Computing and Information Technology (CCIT) was established in 1434 H (2014 G) and offers three programs:

  • 1. Bachelor of Computer Science (BCS)
  • 2. Bachelor of Computer and Network Engineering (BCNE)
  • 3. Bachelor of Information System (BIS)

Field training is a compulsory component for all academic programs at CCIT. It places students in accredited private or public institutions, where they can acquire the practical skills necessary for professional life.

As one of the most effective approaches to experiential learning, field training enables students to strengthen their abilities, apply theoretical knowledge in real-world contexts, and bridge the gap between academic study and professional practice.
