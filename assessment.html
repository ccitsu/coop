Evaluation Components

The student's performance is evaluated based on the following:

1. Company Evaluation

Evaluation by the Field Supervisor at the training organization.

2. Progress Reports

Monthly reports submitted by the student throughout the training period.

3. Field Visit Report

Assessment from the Faculty Advisor's site visit, when possible.

4. Final Report

Comprehensive final report submitted by the student.

5. Presentation

Formal presentation to the examining committee.

Grading Scheme

To pass, a student must receive a minimum score of 60% of the total. The evaluation is conducted on a 100-point scale:

Source of Evaluation Points
Field training organization/supervisor assessment 20
Faculty Advisor assessment (follow up visits & monthly reports) 10
Training Certification (at least two) 40
Final report & presentation (Examining Committee) 30
Total 100
Minimum Passing Grade: You must achieve at least 60 points out of 100 to successfully pass the field training program.

Academic Integrity Policy

Students are expected to adhere to the university's academic integrity policy. This includes:

  • Properly acknowledging all sources of information in their reports.
  • Not collaborating with others on individual assignments.
  • Not falsifying data or citations.
  • Following academic referencing styles when citing external sources.
Academic Integrity Violation: Any violation of academic integrity may result in failure of the field training program and disciplinary action according to university policies.
