Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about the Field Training program.

Students are eligible if they have completed 110 credit hours, including all required core classes, have a cumulative GPA of at least 2.00, and have permission from their department.

The field training is a mandatory 6 to 8-week program with a minimum of 90 total training hours (old plan). Under the new plan, the field training extends to 24 weeks and is typically completed during the summer semester.

No, students are not permitted to take any other credit hours or courses concurrently with the field training. You must be completely free during the training period.

Your final grade is based on a 100-point scale. It is composed of:
  • Field training organization assessment: 20 points
  • Faculty Advisor assessment: 10 points
  • Training Certification (at least two): 40 points
  • Final report and presentation: 30 points
You must achieve a minimum score of 60% to pass the program.

The Faculty Advisor is a key point of contact. They will guide you in setting objectives, approve your work plan, conduct a mandatory site visit to your training organization, and provide feedback on your progress reports and final report.

While the CCIT Training Unit is responsible for helping students find suitable training organizations, you can suggest your own. Any proposed organization must be approved by your department and the CCIT Training Unit.

The Final Report is a detailed document where you reflect on your training experience, tasks, and learning outcomes. The presentation is an opportunity for you to summarize your work and findings to the Field Training Examining Committee. This committee evaluates both the report and the presentation as a major component of your final grade.

Students who fail the field training course must re-enroll and complete the program again in a subsequent semester to fulfill the graduation requirement.

Yes, several forms must be completed. These include the Student Joining Report, Student Progress Reports, and the Final Report. The handbook includes a full list of all standard forms used by students, the training organization, and the university. Visit the Forms & Templates page for more details.

You should immediately contact your Faculty Advisor to discuss any issues. If the problem cannot be resolved, you may request to change your training location within the first two weeks of the program.

Tips for Field Training Students

Be Proactive

Take the initiative to gain useful experience. Don't wait to be told what to do—ask questions and seek opportunities.

Document Everything

Keep an accurate daily/weekly record of your activities. Don't wait until the end to start your final report.

Build Relationships

Network with colleagues and supervisors. These connections can be valuable for your future career.

Communicate Regularly

Maintain regular contact with your Faculty Advisor. Update them on your progress and any challenges.

Link Theory to Practice

Consciously connect what you've learned in the classroom to your practical work. This will enrich your final report.

Represent Well

Remember you are an ambassador of Shaqra University. Demonstrate professionalism, integrity, and punctuality.

Maintain Confidentiality

Respect the confidentiality of any data or information you access during your training.

Seek Feedback

Regularly ask your supervisor for feedback. Use it constructively to improve your performance.

Manage Your Time

Balance your training responsibilities with report writing. Start your final report early.

Prepare Well

Take your final presentation seriously. Practice multiple times before presenting to the committee.
